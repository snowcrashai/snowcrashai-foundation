# Contribution Guidelines

Thank you for your interest in contributing to SnowcrashAI Foundation.

These guidelines exist to ensure contributions remain high-quality, vendor-neutral, and useful to practitioners.

---

## Who can contribute

Contributions are welcome from:
- Security engineers and architects
- Red team and blue team practitioners
- AI/ML engineers with security experience
- Researchers focused on applied AI security

You do not need to be affiliated with an organization to contribute.

---

## What we accept

We welcome contributions including:
- Improvements to existing standards or playbooks
- New AI security risk definitions
- Pentesting and red-teaming methodologies
- Defensive detection and mitigation techniques
- Reference architectures and design patterns
- Research summaries relevant to AI security and assurance

---

## What we do not accept

To preserve focus and credibility, we generally do not accept:
- Vendor marketing material or product promotion
- Paywalled or proprietary-only guidance
- Ethics-only or policy-only discussions without security impact
- Unverified claims without methodology or context
- Exploit code intended for real-world abuse

---

## How to contribute

### 1. Open an issue first (recommended)
Before large changes:
- Open a GitHub issue describing the proposal
- Include scope, motivation, and expected outcome
- Reference related SnowcrashAI artifacts if applicable

This helps avoid duplicate or misaligned work.

### 2. Create a pull request (PR)
When submitting a PR:
- Keep changes focused and scoped
- Use clear, descriptive commit messages
- Reference the related issue (if one exists)

---

## Formatting and structure

- All documents are written in **Markdown**
- Follow existing document structure and headings
- Use clear, concise language suitable for practitioners
- Avoid unnecessary jargon or hype

---

## Versioning

Major artifacts (e.g., Top 10 lists, playbooks) are versioned.
- Breaking or significant changes should increment the version
- Minor clarifications may be merged without version changes
- Version changes should be noted in the relevant document or changelog

## Review process

- Maintainers review contributions for scope, clarity, and alignment
- Feedback may be requested before acceptance
- Not all submissions will be merged; rejection does not imply lack of merit

---

## Code of conduct

All contributors are expected to engage respectfully and professionally.
Harassment, hostility, or disruptive behavior will not be tolerated.

---

## Questions

If you are unsure whether a contribution fits, open an issue and ask.
